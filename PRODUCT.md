# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This site is for broad personal presentation. Visitors may include classmates, tutors, designers, collaborators, friends, and people who encounter Erayan's work through social or professional links. They should quickly understand who Erayan is, what kind of design work he makes, and how to contact him.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Guo Ziyang / Erayan as an industrial designer with a clear personal identity and a concise body of work. The first version should feel like a restrained online exhibition: memorable, calm, and easy to scan, without becoming a dense job-application portfolio.

Success means a visitor can understand Erayan's identity, background, design direction, representative works, and contact path within a short visit.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, spatial, precise.

The emotional tone should be professional and composed, with enough interaction to feel designed rather than static. The site should suggest careful industrial design thinking through pacing, hierarchy, image treatment, and subtle motion.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic portfolio templates, dense resume-style layouts, repeated card grids, cheap glass effects, gradient text, exaggerated rounded cards, decorative visual noise, and animation that distracts from the work. The site should not feel like a job board profile, a heavy academic archive, or an AI-generated landing page.

## Design Principles

1. Let the first screen create a memorable identity through one strong image, minimal type, and controlled contrast.
2. Keep the information lightweight: personal identity first, representative work second, resume details only where they support the story.
3. Use interaction to reveal depth, not to decorate the page.
4. Preserve bilingual clarity: Chinese and English should feel intentional, not duplicated clutter.
5. Build the first version as a light gallery while keeping the structure ready for future project detail pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The site should meet WCAG AA expectations for text contrast and keyboard access. Motion must respect reduced-motion preferences. The mobile experience should remain complete without mouse-based hover interactions, using tap or scroll-based reveal patterns instead.
